Guinness Storehouse

The Guinness Storehouse is a very popular attraction in Dublin, and after visiting it, I understand why! It’s located at St. James’s Gate Brewery, the original home of Guinness since 1759.

The tour is self-guided, so you can take your time walking through the history of Guinness, how it’s made, and how it became one of the most iconic beers in the world. There are interesting interactive displays, vintage ads, and even a room where you can smell the different ingredients. These being barley, hops, yeast, and water. It was actually surprisingly fun to walk around and smell the ingredients. At the end of the tour, you’re also offered a free pint of Guinness.

Drinking culture in Ireland is obviously strong, but I’ve noticed it’s more about the social side than anything else. Having a pint of Guinness in a pub isn’t just about drinking. It’s about talking, laughing, listening to music, and being part of the moment. At least for the Irish people it’s like that. I think the Storehouse captures that feeling really well.
